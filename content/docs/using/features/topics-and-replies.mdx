title: Topics and Replies
description: Start a discussion, reply in a thread, and format your posts with Markdown on a Barazo forum.
---

Discussions on Barazo happen in **topics**. A topic has a title, a first post, and a thread of replies underneath. Topics live inside **categories**, which group them by subject.

## Starting a topic

1. Select **New topic**.
2. Choose a category. Some forums nest categories, so pick the most specific one that fits.
3. Write a title and your post.
4. Publish.

Titles are what people scan in a category list, so a clear one gets more replies than a vague one.

## Formatting with Markdown

Posts use Markdown. You get:

- **Headings, bold, and italics**
- Bulleted and numbered lists
- `inline code` and fenced code blocks
- Links and quotes
- Images, where the forum allows uploads

A live preview shows the rendered result as you type, so you can check formatting before publishing.

## Replying

Open a topic and use the reply box at the bottom. Replies **thread**: a reply attaches to the post it answers, and the conversation nests visually. That keeps a busy topic readable instead of collapsing into one flat list.

To answer a specific reply rather than the topic as a whole, use its own reply action. Your response appears indented beneath it.

## Editing

You can edit your own topics and replies after posting. Because the post is a record in your own account, editing updates that record and the forum re-indexes it.

## Posts held for review

Some forums review new posts before they go public. If yours does, you'll see a note that your topic or reply is awaiting a moderator. It appears to everyone once approved. This is a per-forum setting, and many forums publish immediately.

## Where your posts go

Every topic and reply is written to your AT Protocol account first, then indexed by the forum. You own the original. See [Your account and identity](/docs/using/account-and-identity) for what that means in practice.
