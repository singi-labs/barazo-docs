title: Profiles

---

Your profile is how the forum shows who you are. It pulls from your AT Protocol account, so your handle, display name, and avatar are the same ones you use across the network.

## What's on it

- Your **handle** and **display name**
- Your **avatar** and, where set, a short bio
- Your **activity** on the forum: the topics you've started and the replies you've made

Because your identity comes from your account, editing your display name or avatar on your provider updates how you appear here too.

## Your activity is your reputation

Barazo doesn't invent a score for you. What stands in for reputation is the work itself: the discussions you started, the answers you gave, the reactions those earned. Your profile is a record of what you actually contributed, not a number we assigned.

## Identity that travels

Your profile isn't locked to one forum. The same account carries your identity to every Barazo forum and every other AT Protocol app. Post on one community and move to another, and you're still recognizably you, with the same handle and the same history behind you.

## Editing your profile

Update your profile from your account settings. Some fields come from your provider (handle, display name, avatar) and some are specific to how a forum presents you. The forum shows you which is which.
